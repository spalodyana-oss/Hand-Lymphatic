                    <p>Surgical procedures, especially those involving extensive incisions or fat manipulation (like liposuction, tummy tuck, or spinal surgery), inevitably cause **micro-trauma**. This action severs the delicate network of **initial lymphatic capillaries** and **collecting vessels** essential for drainage.</p>

                    <h3 class="font-semibold text-xl mb-3 accent-text">Inflammation and Edema Formation</h3>
                    <p>The body’s natural immune response triggers inflammation, dramatically increasing capillary permeability. This causes plasma proteins and excess fluid to leak into the interstitial space—a high-protein fluid known as **edema**. The compromised lymph system struggles to clear this fluid load, leading to visible swelling and pressure.</p>
                </div>
                <div class="luxury-card p-5 sm:p-6">
                    <h3 class="font-semibold text-xl mb-3 accent-text">The Stagnation Risk</h3>
                    <p>Post-operative pain and necessary reduced mobility diminish the **Skeletal Muscle Pump**, a key driver of lymph flow. This stagnation not only slows healing but, crucially, allows the high-protein fluid to harden, increasing the risk of painful, restrictive **fibrosis** and delayed tissue repair.</p>
                </div>
            </div>
